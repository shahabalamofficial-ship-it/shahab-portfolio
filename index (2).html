About

I grew up in Nowshera, in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, in a house where Pashto, Urdu, and Punjabi were all spoken before breakfast. My family is now settled in Lahore, and I work in Doha — three cities, one thread running through all of them: I like being useful to people.

For the last three and a half years I've worked in fast food service, starting at KFC in Lahore and now as a Team Member at Go Crispy, a KFC franchise here in Qatar. It's a job most people don't romanticise. I do. There's real craft in staying calm during a rush, in remembering a regular's order, in making sure the person on the other side of the counter feels looked after.

Outside of work, I read philosophy, study psychology, and I'm slowly teaching myself to build with AI — currently learning to work with automation tools so I can build things that help people, not just talk about helping them. I'm also still improving my English, one conversation at a time.

Beyond the counter

What I spend my off-hours thinking about.

I'm teaching myself to build AI automations — WhatsApp agents, workflow tools, small systems that quietly make someone's day easier. I want to master this, not just use it.

I'm drawn to understanding why people feel what they feel. Motivating someone, helping them feel a little stronger or more confident, is one of the things that genuinely makes me happy.

Nietzsche especially. I like ideas that ask hard questions about purpose and self-overcoming — questions I sit with while working through them slowly, in my own words, in my own language.
